Demand Charge Management
i
DCM sizing logic
  • Target Power: Nth-percentile of daily peak load values. This is the level you'd shave demand down to on your worst covered days.
  • Target Energy: Target power × on-peak window length (hours). Enough energy to sustain discharge for the full on-peak period.
  • Viable when on-peak demand charge ≥ $8/kW — otherwise savings don't justify the battery cost.
  • N is set by the Coverage Ambition slider above.
TOU Arbitrage
i
TOU Arbitrage sizing logic
  • Target Energy: Nth-percentile of daily on-peak consumption (kWh). This is how much energy the battery needs to fully offset on-peak consumption on your covered days.
  • Target Power: Target energy ÷ on-peak window length. Sets the inverter power rating needed to deliver that energy within the window.
  • Viable when on-peak minus off-peak energy spread ≥ $0.08/kWh — minimum margin after round-trip losses (~85% RTE).
  • N is set by the Coverage Ambition slider above.
